Headline Valuation

the answer, and the arithmetic behind it

What this is. The estimated equity value range from the primary method (EV/EBITDA). How it's built (5 steps). (1) take listed peers' trading multiples; (2) read the multiple at the target's quality position — its EBITDA-margin percentile in the peer set, so a weaker-margin company earns a lower multiple; (3) × the target's driver → implied enterprise value; (4) − net debt → equity; (5) − DLOM if the company is private (a listed target is already liquid, so DLOM = 0). Low / mid / high span the peer band around the position.

Methodology Validation — not overfit

proof the accuracy generalizes

What this is. Proof the method is accurate in general, not just lucky here. We re-value every listed company in the universe from its peers and compare to its own real market cap. If quality-positioning only worked on the headline target it would be overfitting; instead it must beat the naive "just use the median" approach across the board. corr(margin, EV/EBITDA) confirms the market actually prices quality (near-zero would make positioning meaningless).

VALIDATION: PASS

Error = comps equity vs the company's own market cap, across 32 listed comparables. Positioning is closer on 24/32 targets. Market prices quality: corr(margin, EV/EBITDA) = 0.487.

Valuation Methods — Triangulation

the same answer, checked three ways

What this is. The company valued three independent ways so the answer is corroborated. EV/EBITDA is the headline (capital-structure neutral). EV/Revenue is robust when margins are noisy. EV/EBIT reflects asset intensity via depreciation. For each we take listed comps' multiples, drop outliers with a Tukey 1.5×IQR fence, then read the multiple at the target's quality position with a band around it. A spread across methods is informative, not an error — each captures different economics.

Comparable Peer Set (15)

the companies used to price the target

What this is. The companies used to price the target. How they're chosen. Candidates first pass a hard mismatch filter (same operating model, value chain, D&B major industry), then score 0–1 on five weighted dimensions: industry 40%, scale 20%, margin 15%, customer 15%, export 10% — shown as chips on each card. Not every peer is an exact match. Each peer's multiple is similarity-weighted (w on the card): a full match (score ≥ 0.85) counts fully, a borderline one tapers toward a 0.15 floor, so a set padded with loose comps cannot distort the answer. Here 0 of 15 peers are borderline → effective peer count 15.0. Only listed peers contribute trading multiples.
