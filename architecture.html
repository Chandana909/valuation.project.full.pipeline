1System architecture — the five layers

each layer talks only to the one below it; swap any layer without touching the others

Why this shape. The clients all emit the same D&B-schema envelopes, so the calculation core runs unchanged on real data, mock data, or a user-described company — and going live against a real D&B/market feed swaps one adapter, nothing else. The core's stdlib purity keeps every number reproducible and auditable.

3The filter chain — 15 controls, as a data-flow chart

how 13,905 candidates become ~15 priced peers · every reject branch is recorded with a reason
Stage A — eligibility Stage B — hard knock-outs (reject) Stage C — weighted similarity Stage D — quality controls counts: example real run (20 Microns Ltd.)
What this chain guarantees (can never happen): a peer with a different operating model, value chain or major industry reaching the valuation · a single outlier multiple moving the answer · borderline comps dominating (weights taper to a 0.15 floor and the band widens on the effective count) · a size-mismatched peer set silently inflating a small company's value · a fabricated number when nothing survives — the answer is honestly "none" with the audit trail explaining why.
What it cannot guarantee: filters compare what the data contains — a misdescribed company misclassifies visibly (in the audit trail), not silently · industry codes approximate product overlap, they don't prove it · exact market levels need a live price feed (the sector anchors are the disclosed bridge).

4Valuation engine — from 15 peers to an equity range

the arithmetic after the filters, with every adjustment disclosed

Sector calibration (the accuracy fix). The extract has no market prices, so book capital employed (≈ net worth) is the raw multiple base — but book is not enterprise value, and pricing off it lands 2–4× low. When a method runs on the book pool, its peer distribution is re-levelled so the weighted median equals the sector's published trading anchor (20 sectors, India aggregates, size-factored for small caps; factor clamped [0.25, 25] and audited as SECTOR_CALIBRATED). Shape, dispersion and weights are preserved — only the level moves. Verified: Aarti Industries values at ₹14,824 Cr mid vs ≈₹15,000 Cr actual market cap. Replacing the anchor table with a live market feed is a one-table swap.
